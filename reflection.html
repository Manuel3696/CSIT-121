What I Have Learned

Reflections on my journey through web development this semester.

Challenges and Triumphs

This final project was rewarding and challenging at the same time. One of the most satisfying factors was witnessing all of the work I've completed throughout the semester come together and form one, fully cohesive portfolio. The challenge was to design them all so that the pages each had their own function but remain consistently designed.

Planning and layout decisions do indeed help or hinder user experience and not having any experience designing labs before this project, I had to reallocate much of the original lab work to create a better fit for the structure of the site. It was great to have applied all I have learned and to have a site I can be proud of.

Observations on Testing Multiple Browsers and Devices.

Testing on multiple browsers (Chrome and Firefox) and testing it on my phone gave me an insight into what would happen across browsers and seen how the site would reflect itself on a mobile device. I've noticed a little bit differences in margins and paddings rendering from it on Firefox.

While on mobile I realized the navigation bar was not readable and not easily accessible with touch. All of these differences encouraged me to hone my media queries and really get into the layout behavior to make my site more flexible and beneficial for my users.
